# BigSet teaser v2 — "Generative Loom / Pattern House" prompts

Same narrative as v1 (scattered fragments → invisible force organizes them → wordmark), new world: warp threads, shuttles, Jacquard punch cards, selvedge inspection, woven cloth. Palette: warm ivory light, undyed linen/ecru, deep indigo thread, graphite black iron heddles, brass fittings, muted beige sleeve. Hands only, never faces. Overall look: mid-century textile-mill archive, procedural and forensic.

Workflow per image-to-video.md: generate the start-frame image for clip 01, render, extract exact last frame, use it as clip 02's start frame, and so on. Clip = 6 s, 24 fps, 16:9. Motion prompts describe motion only, never a new scene.

---

## Clip 01 — The pattern house is revealed (fade-up, overhead)

**Start-frame image prompt:**
Perfectly overhead top-down view of a large backlit worktable in a mid-century textile pattern house, emerging from near-darkness. Arranged orthogonally around the perimeter: skeins of indigo and ecru yarn, wound wooden bobbins, a stack of Jacquard punch cards bound with twine, folded swatches of woven cloth, fabric shears, a wooden shuttle, brass thread gauges, kraft envelopes with typewritten labels, a magnifying loupe. At center, one large rectangle of deep indigo cloth — the strongest color block. Warm ivory light, low exposure, cinematic, photorealistic, 16:9.

**Motion prompt:** Exposure rises smoothly from dark to fully lit, like a lightbox warming up. Camera holds perfectly centered overhead with an extremely slow push-in. No object moves. No cuts.

## Clip 02 — The classified pattern card

**Motion prompt (start frame = last frame of clip 01):** Hard visual settle onto the table, then a beige-sleeved hand enters from the bottom edge and slowly lifts a single Jacquard punch card from the stack, holding it flat above the table so its grid of punched holes catches the backlight. Slow, deliberate movement. Camera pushes in gently toward the card. No cuts.

## Clip 03 — Fragments accumulate (stop-motion archive)

**Motion prompt:** Punch card lowers back down; in quick stop-motion jumps, new specimens populate the table surface one by one: a swatch of herringbone cloth, a card wound with graded indigo threads, a paper diagram of a weave draft, a small brass reed comb, a photograph of cloth under magnification. Each appears with a slight settle. Camera drifts slowly across the growing arrangement. No cuts.

## Clip 04 — Thread under tension (macro)

**Motion prompt:** Camera dives down into extreme macro on parallel warp threads stretched under tension, shallow depth of field, individual fibers glowing in warm backlight like a granular landscape. The camera glides along the threads; a shuttle slides through in soft focus in the background, lifting alternate threads in a slow wave. No cuts.

## Clip 05 — Measurement and inspection

**Motion prompt:** Pull back to a clean frontal view; a hand raises a magnifying loupe over the woven cloth while thin annotation lines and small measurement labels animate in from the sides, connecting to the weave structure like a technical catalog diagram. A brass thread gauge rotates slowly beside it. Precise, linear camera push-in. No cuts.

## Clip 06 — The typed thesis

**Motion prompt:** The scene transitions as a warm brown typewritten page slides up to fill the frame, reading as an archival research note with three small ink diagrams beneath the text labeled INTERLACE, TENSION, SETTLING, showing thread paths bending over, against, and along each other. Camera slowly pushes in on the diagrams. Warm paper tone. No cuts.

*(If your tool supports text rendering, put this on the page: "A dataset can behave like a loom. Scattered threads become tension. Tension becomes interlace. Interlace becomes pattern. Pattern becomes settled cloth.")*

## Clip 07 — The numeral tease

**Motion prompt:** The typed page falls away to white; a single Jacquard punch card rises to center frame, and its punched holes are backlit so brightly they resolve into a large glowing indigo numeral 3 formed from the dot grid. The card holds centered, dots pulsing subtly with light. Minimal camera movement. No cuts.

## Clip 08 — Chaos of loose thread (fast, aggressive)

**Motion prompt:** Hard energy shift: a tangle of loose indigo and black threads erupts from the center toward the lens with strong radial motion blur, filling the frame — the film's fastest moment. Then the tangle begins to pull taut, threads straightening into parallel lines under tension. High contrast black-on-ivory. No cuts.

## Clip 09 — Threads settle into the woven mark

**Motion prompt:** The taut parallel threads begin weaving themselves in accelerating stop-motion: a shuttle flies left-right, rows of cloth build upward, and the emerging fabric carries a symmetric radial pattern woven in indigo — loops and lobes around a dark circular core, like a field diagram rendered in thread. Camera slowly pulls back and centers the finished motif. Motion decelerates to near-stillness. No cuts.

## Clip 10 — Identity resolve

**Motion prompt:** The woven radial motif slowly fades and unravels into the ivory background, threads thinning away; at center, small experimental letterforms appear and morph in quick succession — stitched cursive, punch-card dot letters, stencil glyphs — before resolving into a final bold uppercase BIGSET wordmark in clean dark sans-serif, holding centered and still on the pale ivory field for the last two seconds. No cuts.

*(If the generator can't render the wordmark cleanly, end on the empty ivory field after the unravel and composite the wordmark in post — you have wordmark.png from v1's process.)*
